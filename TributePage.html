Designation of Pluto as a Dwarf Planet

Since it's discovery in 1930 until 2006, pluto was known as the 9th planet in the solar system. As more information was discovered about Pluto, this small solar satellite appeared to have less and less in common with the other plants. During this time additional objects orbiting the Sun were being discovered. These new objects appeared to have more in common with Pluto than the 8 other planets. They are large enough to be spheroid in shape, but not large enough to have cleared out the objects within their orbit. In 2006 the decision was made to classify Pluto and a few other objects as as dwarf planets.
Current List of Dwarf Planets
  • Pluto
  • Ceres
  • Eris
  • Haumea
  • Makemake

Negative Reaction from the Public

Even though Pluto's assignment was changed from the 9th planet to the first object designated as a dwarf planet, the public reaction has had a fair share of criticism. The change in classification has been viewed as a "demotion" for Pluto by some.
